An Endangered Species

The giant panda is an endangered species. There are fewer than 1,000 left living in the wild. The greatest threat to this species is habitat loss. Large areas of bamboo forests are cleared to accomodate China's human population. Also, although greater awareness about this species' endangered status has minimized poaching, this is still a major threat to the giant panda's survival due to its small numbers.

Panda Facts:

  • Unlike other bears, pandas do not hibernate.
  • The panda's average lifespan in the wild is around 20 years.
  • Although they spend most of their time on the ground, pandas can swim and climb trees.
 
 

How You Can Help

Get Involved At Your Local Zoo

Since there are so few pandas left living in the wild, those in captivity also play a big role in protecting the future of the species. Zoos worldwide care for pandas, breed them, and help to spread awareness about their endangered status. You can help by donating time and/or money to your local zoo. Click here to read more suggestions from the Adelaide Zoo in Australia about how you can help the giant panda..

Promote Conservation

Habitat destruction is the biggest threat to the wild giant panda's survival. There are many organizations, such as The World Wildlife Fund and Pandas International, who focus on, among other things, helping animals and humans co-habitate without unneccesary destruction of animal habitats. They work with governments, help local populations to find animal and environment friendly ways of living and earning incomes, and provide support to parks and preserves. By donating to these organizations, you support the work they do to help the giant panda and other endangered species.
